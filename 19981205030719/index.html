 About AustNet
Thousands of people around the world connect through the Internet to the Relay Chat, a popular communication method of the 20th century. Originating in 1988, the concept of IRC has grown to the forefront of Internet participation and continues to expand alongside the information technology revolution.

AustNet, an Internet Relay Chat network, is the alternative to lag, splits and channel takeovers that plague other IRC networks. AustNet is based upon the principle of an efficient, secure and well developed network that allows users to own their channels and actively participate in the growth of an international network. Founded in Australia, AustNet has rapidly expanded through Canada, Singapore, New Zealand and the United States, providing international coverage, with high quality servers. AustNet features minimal lag and netsplits along with exceptional public services.

Alongside AustNet's efficient servers, the network provides users with the ability to register their channels and nicknames. Featuring superior technical innovations, AustNet excels in client services and online help. For more information browse About AustNet.
