This page has descriptions of some of the AustNet channels, both official and user-managed, and links to more information.

 #30&40's
#30&40's has grown along with Austnet, to be a modestly popular channel of Austnet. We at #30&40's try to have a relaxed atmosphere, where people can have some fun, make new friends and share experiences of different cultures around the world. The clean and healthy content and helping atmosphere have seen a lot of good friendships and in some cases relationships evolve. So feel free to stop on by, relax and enjoy some of what Austnet is all about.

 #beginner
The #Beginner channel on Austnet has a long history of friendliness and helpfulness. It is one of the few chat/help channels where you can use any language you wish and any colour configuration or client you desire. This channel is rated "G" meaning it's a channel for the entire family, a channel you can let your kids chat on, closely supervised by our team of operators. If you wish to gain op experience this is the place to go: we cater to you the user. Anyone is welcome, beginners and experts alike, so come on down and say "hi" to us, we don't bite (:

 #chit_chat
#Chit_Chat is a great place to relax and have a casual chat. Anyone is welcome at #Chit_Chat, providing you follow a few simple rules: no harassment, no flooding, and no advertising (these are Austnet rules anyway, so they shouldn't be any problem!). #Chit_Chat is run by Scapegoat, Cassie, and Jediz, who are all IRC-connoisseurs. So if you happen to run into any IRC trouble, just pop into #Chit_Chat and ask around. Next time you're on IRC join #Chit_Chat, then sit back, relax, and enjoy a chit-chat!

 #mirc
#mIRC is a software support channel for the mIRC client. We will gladly answer any questions about it as well as questions pertaining to general IRC and AustNet services. We will NOT, however, answer any questions about war scripting or hacking. Should you find yourself infected with a mIRC oriented virus, come by; we have removal information for most viruses found on IRC.

 #soundbar
We are an international, virtual family-style tavern. Getting our start on IRCnet in July of '96 as #Ircbar2, we became IRC's original sound file trading channel. Moving to Austnet in August of '97, we brought with us a full service channel. Besides the friendly cast of channel regulars and operators, we offer a bar with a hot tub, jukebox, and dance floor; drinks by Barstool the bartender; fine food from DeeDee's kitchen; weddings by the RevMorgan; a channel newsletter by Domi; help with ascii art and popups; and of course, the best tunes on IRC!

  • A paragraph for this page
  • A larger description/advertisement page to be stored on this site
  • The address of any official channel web site
  • A contact email address (preferably that of the manager).

We reserve the right to modify or reject any submission on the following grounds:

  • Bad HTML
  • Bad English
  • Large or badly-formatted graphics
  • Content judged inappropriate for the AustNet web site

If you don't supply graphics you'll get the world-famous "AustNet Look" Garrison Sans headings. We suggest you supply some.
