AustNet reaches 60th place on Orca
Austnet's project-orca.net team is now ranked 60th with more than 9 millions keys and only 14 members. This is an increase of 10 places in just one week. If you would like to become part of this effort, join the team today!

AustNet today launched a new mailing list for discussion related to Public Relations. Anyone with ideas on raising the profile of AustNet in a positive way, or who would like to give input, can join the list by sending email to publicrelations-subscribe@austnet.org.
AustNet reaches 70th on project-orca.net
Some time in the past few days AustNet achieved a rank of 70 with 7,394,860 keys on project-orca. For those of you that aren't aware, project orca is the followup to project dolphin, a project whos sole purpose is to count keystrokes of its participants.
On Project Dolphin the AustNet team reached a rank of 25, with over 200 million keys in total. Project Orca is still in the development phase but they have implemented some anti-cheating features already .. hopefully with time we can surpass our old stats for Project Dolphin! If you wish to join the austnet team go HERE and sign up for an account. Download the client from the download page, login, go to your account then join/change teams and select AustNet from the list.
  Unfortunately project-orca has not currently a linux client, or their statistics engine fully implemented. Until this is done you can check your teams total through the "your account" page. Congrats to AustNet and happy typing to those who join!
15th March, 2003, Submitted by Karl
AustNet 10th place on Netsplit.de
Netsplit.de ranks IRC Networks based on a combination of factors including average number of channels, average number of users and average number of servers. Austnet last night reached number 10 on the listing!
  The netsplite.de website polls networks every 3 or so hours. In the past few weeks AustNet has added one server, and its peak has risen by almost 1000 users. This is partially due to the misfortune which has befallen other networks in the form of continuous DDoS attacks, and also due to AustNets recent efforts to improve its public image. Congratulations to AustNet on this achievement!
14th March, 2003, Submitted by Karl

Austnet team started at Project Orca
31st January, 2003, Submitted by karl
Project-dolphin, the key stroke counting project born from curiosity, ended on the 17th of January. Shortly after, Project-orca was created to follow in its footsteps. Feel free to join the new Austnet team if you signup.
 
Dalnet under attack
28th January, 2003, Submitted by hippychick
The irc network Dalnet has been under continuous dDos attack for the past few weeks. As a result, many users have been unable to connect, and have explored alternative networks, including AustNet. Many have decided to stay, and we have produced an "ex-Dalnet user's guide" to help their transition to AustNet.
