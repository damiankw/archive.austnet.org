Common Ident

By lolly 0 Comments
Categories: Support

One of the most comment nickop errors that everyone gets, is ident being too common. This is generally in place because of various trojans/virii/clones/drones/proxies and of course spambots.

We have a list below of the common idents that will prevent you from either registering, or identifying to your nicknames.

In order to change your ident, you need to do the following:

These instructions are for mIRC users.

Type //identd on $me then /quit the IRC server and then rejoin, this should fix the problem.

However sometimes it may have something to do with your email that has been set. If that’s the case, you need to fix that aswell. This can be done with this command in mIRC.

/emailaddr email@address.ok

Changes the email address in the Connect dialog - then you can disconnect from the server with /quit and then reconnect to the server and hopefully you would be able to change your ident.

denial of service attacks

By admin 0 Comments
Categories: Servers

we have recently suffered Distributed Denial of Service Attacks (DDoS) which increases netsplits and degrades performance of the IRC servers – this in the majority of cases is the reason why you will experience delay, netsplits or loss of connection with our network.  this results from several known users launching attacks against our IRC servers;  we are currently working with the Australian High Tech Crime Centre (AHTCC), a division of the Australian Federal Police (AFP), NSW Police E-Crime Unit, Queensland Police Computer Crime Investigation Unit (CGIU), South Australia Police Electronic Crime Section (ECS), Western Australia Police Computer Crime Investigation Team and the Victorian Computer Crime Squad to bring such users to court and criminal punishment, as this activity is illegal and now monitored by the majority of Internet Service Providers (ISPs) around the country and world. 

moreover, we are also working very closely with major service providers to trace the origins of DDoS attacks using packet capturing and filtering on their core routers for users launching such attacks.  check out the law’s latest catch on http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4EryVTX6w3I

#AVA - Virus Help Channel

By lolly 0 Comments
Categories: Support

#AVA is one of our unique features of AustNet, AustNet’s virus help channel.

#AVA was first registered Sat Oct 31 1998 and has been going strong ever since.

AVA is a great team of volunteer helpers who are available to assist any IRC/Austnet users that might be infected by trojans/worms and other nasties. #AVA has helped many IRC/Austnet users over the 9 years it has been operating and has made available removals, tutorials and help files to assist in the removal process.

HELP !! HELP !! I am infected ….

If you ever feel you have become infected with a virus and join #AVA for help, please be patient and polite, chances are we are busy helping someone else and whilst we might not immediately respond to your first panicked screams, someone will be with you as soon as possible. Please remember it is your responsibility to keep your computer clean and there are many free Anti-virus programs available on the Internet for your use.

Just ask your AVA helper, we are here to assist.

Over all, we are a friendly bunch of ops, always happy to help.

Want to help?
If you are over 17, have an urge to help people, have the patience of Job and have a good knowledge of mIRC seasoned with some scripting savvy .. why not apply to join us ?? The pay may not be great but the company’s grand.

Registering and Identifying Nicknames

By lolly 0 Comments
Categories: Services

As AustNet now has mode +R, many channels have set it, so that only registered users who have identified to NickOP can access these channels. We have had many questions from users who do not know how to use services, how to register their nicks, and how to identify to them.

To REGISTER your nickname you must follow the instructions below. Please keep in mind that these are instructions with examples.

1: Think of a password, and write it down somewhere safe.
The password must be minimum 6 characters long, with a mix of numbers and letters

2: Use your real email address.
If you lose your password for your registered nick, ASD staff can send the password direct to your email address, with instructions on how to identify to your nickname.

3: Register your nickname.
If you are using mIRC, then please ensure you follow these instructions, and look at the screenshots below.

Query nickop to read the instructions.

Type /query nickop help

STEP 1 CLICK HERE

Switch to the Main AustNet Status screen, nickop will send you notices, and look for the instructions. Then type out your command to register, as seen below we have used:

/msg nickop REGISTER purpler41n immonfire@gmail.com || THIS IS AN EXAMPLE ONLY

STEP 2 CLICK HERE

When you press enter, confirmation shows up in the Status screen that your nick is registered, the host, and that you have set mode +R

STEP 3 CLICK HERE

Now you can join any channel you wish.

In addition, we do have many users who keep registering new nicknames, due to the current message being sent

EG: #sydney:You need a registered nick to join that channel, please see #ASD.

This is leading to many users registering excessive nicknames, which is unnecessary. Users of Austnet are permitted only 4 nicknames per PERSON. ASD staff members are now deleting excessive nick registrations, and placing bans on addresses to stop further registration of nicks, and a message for the user to come into #ASD to learn how to identify.

What users need to do, is to remember to IDENTIFY as the nicknames they have set up previously.

You need to type the following when you next log into AustNet, otherwise you can’t join registered channels:

Ensure you are on the AustNet Status screen, and then attempt your IDENTIFICATION.

/msg nickop@austnet.org IDENTIFY purpler41n||THIS IS AN EXAMPLE ONLY

STEP 4 CLICK HERE

Once you have entered in your username and password, you should get a message from nickop that would look similar below, and a line that states mode: +R has been set.

STEP 5 CLICK HERE

If you find you are still having difficulties, feel free to join #ASD and ask questions from our helpers.
