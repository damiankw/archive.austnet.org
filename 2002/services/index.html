AustNet provides several technical services designed to enhance the user experience and provide features to ensure network security. The operator services automate and manage channel and nickname registrations. For futher information on any of the services listed below, simply click on the service nickname:
  • ChanOP (Channel Registration)
    The channel operator server allows users to register their channels and maintain a secure method of issuing operator status. More than a mere "bot", ChanOP is a server, effectively allowing it to maintain every aspect of your channel according to your wishes.

    Registered channels are listed in the Channel Registra web page.
  • NickOP (Nickname Registration)
    The nickname operator server controls who has authorisation to use certain nicknames. Upon connecting to any AustNet server, your nickname is validified with NickOP and if it is not yours, you are given the option to authenticate yourself or be disconnected. NickOP has speedy registration facilities and secure ownership enforcement.

    Registered nicknames are listed in the Nickname Registra web page.
  • NoteOP (Message Bank)
    The message bank server allows you to leave notes to registered users (with NickOP) even when they are offline. Your notes are kept under the strict confidentiality and require your password to be viewed.
  • OperOP (Operator Service)
    The operator service allows administration and authorised IRC Operators to maintain the network, using functions that are defined by their access. It has relatively few commands, since it offers only services that do not fit into the public genre of ChanOP and NickOP.

    Authorised administrative patrons are listed in the Administration Registra
