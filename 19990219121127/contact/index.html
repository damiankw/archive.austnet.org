There are various methods of contacting AustNet based upon who the audience of your request should be. Simply click on the any of the following mail links to contact AustNet:

Public Forum Mailing List

The user mailing list is an open public list available for AustNet clients. This mailing list is the main discussion forum for the management of the network, user requests and general discussion.

  • Individual requests should be simply mailed to austnet@austnet.org.

  • Permanent subscription to austnet@austnet.org should be done via mailing majordomo@austnet.org, with body of "subscribe austnet".

Support Department

For assistance with any AustNet related problems or issues, you can mail the AustNet Support Department and the network will reply as soon as possible. Topics normally include connection details, service assistance and general network information. It is estimated your post will be replied within one hour or less during normal business hours.

  • Individual help requests should be simply mailed to help@austnet.org.

  • Permanent subscription to help@austnet.org should be done via mailing majordomo@austnet.org, with body of "subscribe help".

  • Head of Department: Phillip Pudney

Services Department

The AustNet Services Department maintains a support service for the questions and discussion specifically related to AustNet Services. This entails recommendations, enhancement notifications, channel and nickname registration disputes and/or mediation.

  • Individual help requests should be simply mailed to services@austnet.org.

  • Permanent subscription to services@austnet.org should be done via mailing majordomo@austnet.org, with body of "subscribe services".

  • Head of Department: seecs@austnet.org

Network Topology and Routing

The Routing Department discusses and manages AustNet's topology and server connectivity. It is responsible for server applications, maintenance and announcements.

  • Individual help requests should be simply mailed to routing@austnet.org.

  • Permanent subscription to services@austnet.org should be done via mailing majordomo@austnet.org, with body of "subscribe routing".

Web/FTP Site Development

The AustNet Web Site Department manages AustNet's web pages and FTP site.

  • Individual requests should be simply mailed to website@austnet.org.

  • Head of Department: Phillip Pudney

Public Relations

The Public Relations Department is the point of contact for media and corporations wishing to obtain more information on AustNet. This department handles celebrity chat events, media releases and can show you what AustNet can do for your company.

  • Individual requests should be simply mailed to pr@austnet.org.

  • Head of Department: Phillip Pudney

Abuse Mediation

AustNet encourages users who may be concerned about the actions of official AustNet staff to e-mail the abuse response service. The board of will resolve any concerns or issues you may have in a fair and independant manner, making sure that AustNet remains one of the best IRC networks around.

Requests should be simply mailed to abuse@austnet.org with relevant information and logs.
