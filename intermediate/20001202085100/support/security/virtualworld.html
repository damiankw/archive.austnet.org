VirtualWorld
Introduction
Although VirtualWorld is not a foolproof solution which will put a stop to nukes once and for all, it will not allow any ICMP traffic or nukes to pass to your PC if it is used correctly. VirtualWorld effectively does this by failing to provide information to nukers that is essential in order for them to nuke your PC. However, this will not stop you or your IRC client from providing this kind of information.

Using VirtualWorld Effectively

  • Make sure DCC auto-accept is turned off. This can be located by going to DCC, Options... in mIRC's menubar and pressing the Show get dialogue and Show chat dialogue radio buttons in the Send and Chat tabs.
  • Disable your Identd Server which located in mIRC's setup screen.
  • Disable your Fserve script or command.
  • Turn off mIRC's DCC server.
  • Do NOT listen for "!nick file get requests", which can be changed through File, Options, Sound in mIRC's menubar.
  • NEVER tell anyone your IP address.
  • Don't type /mode yournick -v. This will turn VirtualWorld off and let anyone obtain your IP address through /whois, /who and various other commands.
