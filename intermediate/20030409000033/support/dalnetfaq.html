How do I change my password?
/msg nickop@austnet.org set password newpasshere
eg /msg nickop@austnet.org set password d0nnerkeb4b

How do I change my email address?
/msg nickop set email newemail@address.com

Hey how come you didn't use nickop@austnet.org on that last command?
Because the @austnet.org is advised for any command that includes your password so people can't try to steal your nick.

Can I have two people with 200 in my channel?
In short, no. Austnet does not currently allow more than one channel owner.

How can I add someone to my channel?
/msg chanop adduser #chan nick level
They will have to have a registered nick and the maximum level is 199
eg: /msg chanop adduser #mynewchan fre4kyb0y 150

What commands can I do at a certain level?
This can be found out by typing /msg chanop help levels.
If you wish to know which commands you can use in a certain channel type /msg chanop commands #channel

How do I invite myself to a channel?
/msg chanop invite #channel
ChanOP will invite you to the channel. You must have access in the channel to use this command.

How do I unban myself?
If you have access, and same or higher level than the person who banned you, you can type
/msg chanop unban #channel me
If you do not, you will have to wait out the ban, or talk to one of the channel operators.

How do I ban someone from my channel?
This can be done by
/msg chanop ban #chan nick reason
There are lots of options which can be used with the ban command. For a list of them read /msg chanop help ban

What do I do if I forget my password?
In the event you forget your password you will need to join #ASD and ask one of their helpers to help you.

What channels do I have access in?
If you need to know what channel you have access in ask in #ASD for a clist. These are given out at a maximum of one per day. (This doesn't mean one should be requested each day.)

How can I find out more?
While you are online, general questions can be answered in #help, and services questions in #asd. You can also find out more information in the online help for each service:
/msg chanop help
/msg nickop help
/msg noteop help
/msg loveop help


For more help, you can ask in #help, or contact help@austnet.org. For services help, contact ASD helpers in #asd, or email services@austnet.org.


Like in any move you can expect change, but it's not always bad! One of the many great things you will grow to love about AustNet are the channel and nickname Services! (ChanOP & NickOP). Everyone is encouraged to read
/msg ChanOP HELP & /msg NickOP HELP

Unlike the DALnet Access System - ChanServ, where you
have access levels of AOP, SOP and Founder, on AustNet the Channel Access System ChanOP allows for much greater control, using access levels between 1 and 200. For a list of what each access level represents, read
/msg ChanOP help levels

If you haven't already had a look at all the great new options AustNet Services allow you to set on your channel (such as Fun Messages and the ability to have Services email you complete logs of all services commands executed by channel operators), then you should have a read of
/msg ChanOP HELP Set

Sending or receiving Memos (Notes) on AustNet is just as easy as doing so on DALnet! AustNet provides a service called NoteOP, which is much like
MemoServ on DALnet. For more information on using NoteOP, read
/msg NoteOP HELP
