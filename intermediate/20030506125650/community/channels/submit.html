Is your channel friendly to newcomers? Would you like to submit your channel on the "Channel of the Week" page? If you would, send us your channel profile today! (See instructions below).

Criteria
Your channel must fit the following to be featured:

Friendly to new people;
More than 3 months old;
Not have been suspended in the last 6 months;
Channels with a good reputation on AustNet are given precedence.

What to submit
Write and send a 400-500 word profile in a plain text file (.txt extension) to pr@austnet.org. The text file should be named yourchannelname.txt (eg trivia.txt). You can also submit a logo/ad for your channel - max. 200 x 220 pixels, in .gif or .jpg format. This also should be named the same as your channel (eg: trivia.jpg) and it should be attached to the same email as your channel profile.
 
Suggestions for what information you could include in your profile:
What sort of channel it is (scripting, general chat, sex chat, age-based, trivia, etc), interesting things that have happened in the channel, the history of the channel (has the channel changed owners? anything else interesting in the channel's history?), where the channel's name comes from, average number of users in the channel, and anything else you think may attract people to your channel.
 
You should also include the following information in point form:
Channel Name
Slogan for the channel (this will appear in bold at the top of the page)
Channel Website URL (if you have one)
Owner's nickname (and email address)
Creation date:
Number of ops:
Number of bans:
Recommended Age Group:
Type of channel:
 
Points To Note:
If you are not the owner, you must have the owner's permission to submit the profile for Featured Channel of the Week. We will check this and any other information in the file before we go ahead and feature your channel.
Channel profiles submitted in any other format other than .txt will be returned unused.
We reserve the right to reject any profile.
Channels are not necessarily used in the order they are received. AustNet retains editorial control over when the profiles are used.
Profiles may be editted to improve readability or length.
