Channel of the Week:  

  Our ops come from different Australian cities and different countries. All have varied experience in life and on IRC and are sure to entertain at times. Unlike many capital city channels on AustNet, #sydney does not ban countries and domains as a rule. Whilst we are an Australian city, the channel reflects the city itself; a meeting place for people of different cultures and ideas.
  It's now getting to the point where we pretty much have chat going 24/7. The times there is no chat, everyone has probably passed out drunk. We encourage users to come and say hello in #sydney and make new friends. Our rules are simple, and #Sydney's only language is
English.

With all that said, on behalf of the ops team and users of #Sydney, we say "Welcome!"

#Sydney  

Reported by Lolly

#Sydney of the last 2 years has grown from a small - medium channel of perhaps 50 people a night, to a large buzzing community of an average of 250 each night.
 
  It's had a chequered past, with different owners and the infamous sydney2000 bot (which wouldn't let anybody into the channel at all). The current owner of #sydney is ryan, one of the CEO's of AustNet. He sometimes visits but leaves the management to maddog, with 2 Managers, FemmeFatale and myself, lolly.
   #Sydney prides itself on its ability to attract people who wish to chat. We encourage discussion and topical debates at times, however there are times when topics get very heated - everyone is an expert, everyone has an opinion and everyone needs to cool off! #sydney is a chat channel, if you want to hook up, find a chick, find a guy, then #sydney is not the place for you.
   #Sydney is a very social channel, we have channel meets every few months. Individuals are free to (and they do) organise spontaneous events to attend and invite others along. These are usually announced, discussed and debated in our #sydney forums. These forums are also used by some people as a place to socialise ;-).
